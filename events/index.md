# Events

QANTA has been running human vs. computer competitions and workshops since 2015.

## Timeline

**2025**
QANTA 2025: Human–AI Cooperative QA. First competition to focus on human–AI teaming alongside traditional human vs. computer competition.

**2024**
Human vs. Computer Open.

**2023**
Human vs. Computer Open — July 23, University of Maryland. Supported by Meta. Three tracks: question writing, human teams, computer systems. ([Details](/competition/2023/))

**2021**
CMSC 470 class students competed against trivia experts using a GPT-based system.

**2020**
NeurIPS 2020 Efficient QA competition — evaluating how rapidly computer systems answered questions compared to trivia experts.

**May 2019**
UMD undergraduate students competed against Jeopardy! All-Stars.

**End of 2018**
Full tournament at University of Maryland. Human and computer teams competed head-to-head; "Rage Against the Machine" claimed first place. ([Results](http://users.umiacs.umd.edu/~jbg/qb/2018_QANTA/2018_qanta_games.html)) Co-located with NeurIPS 2018. Also featured the "Trick Me 2" adversarial question writing event.

**June 2018**
Collaborated with OUSIA on a "Trick Me" competition where writers crafted questions designed to stump the QANTA system.

**2017**
Returned to HSNCT for competition against quiz bowl experts. Organized the NIPS 2017 competition, won by OUSIA.

**2016**
Competed against top quiz bowlers at HSNCT. Organized community-wide competition where a high school team faced the OUSIA winner.

**2015**
System debuted at HSNCT; later played against Ken Jennings at the University of Washington. Coverage: *"Question-Answering System Built by UMD, UC Boulder Bests Ken Jennings."*
